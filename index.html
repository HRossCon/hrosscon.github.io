What is D&D?

D&D is a tabletop role-playing game where players create characters and go on adventures. Players describe the actions they want their characters to perform and then roll special dice to decide what happens. The game is overseen by a person known as the Dungeon Master, or DM for short. The DM's role is to determine what happens to the players' characters based on their abilites and the outcome of the dice rolls. The DM also controls any non-player characters (NPCs) and the monsters and villains the players fight.

The players' characters go on many adventures designed by the DM. One adventure might have the players exterminate a horde of zombies in an undergound crypt while the next might involve finding a lost magical artifact of immense power. It's all up to the creativity of the DM. As characters complete objectives and defeat enemies, they level up, gaining new powers and abilities that allow them to face more dangerous threats. Many adventures make up an overarching campaign which might last for months or years of real time. Adventuring is inherently dangerous, however, and player characters can be killed by powerful foes or dastardly traps. When this happens, a player can create a new character. If all the player characters die, it is known as a total party kill (TPK) and might signal the end of one campaign and the start of a new one.

Class Options

Every player character in D&D has at least one class. A character's class determines what weapons and armor they can use, what spells they can cast (if any), and any special abilities they might have. The table below lists the classes available in the SRD.

Character Class Hit Die Armor Martial Weapons Spellcasting Special Feature
Barbarian d12 Light, Medium Yes No Rage
Bard d8 Light Some Yes Inspiration
Cleric d8 Light, Medium No Yes Divine Intervention
Druid d8 Light, Medium Some Yes Wild Shape
Fighter d10 Light, Medium, Heavy Yes Yes* Second Wind
Monk d8 None Some No Ki Points
Paladin d10 Light, Medium, Heavy Yes Yes Smite
Ranger d10 Light, Medium Yes Yes Natural Explorer
Rogue d8 Light Some Yes** Sneak Attack
Sorcerer d6 None None Yes Metamagic
Warlock d8 Light None Yes Pact Magic
Wizard d6 None None Yes Spellbook

*Fighters can cast spells if they choose the Eldritch Knight subclass at level 3.
**Rogues can cast spells if they choose the Arcane Trickster subclass at level 3.
